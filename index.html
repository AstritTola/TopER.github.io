Abstract

Graph embeddings are central to graph representation learning, yet most methods rely on high-dimensional, opaque representations that limit interpretability. We introduce Topological Evolution Rate (TopER), a novel 2D topology-inspired embedding that captures filtration evolution via simple linear regression. TopER offers strong performance for classification and clustering, yields highly interpretable visualizations, and scales to large datasets.

Overview

TopER decomposes each graph into a sequence of filtration subgraphs, recording node and edge growth. A linear model L(x) = a + b x fitted to this evolution produces two interpretable quantities:

  • Pivot (a) â€“ captures baseline connectivity
  • Growth (b) â€“ describes how edges evolve relative to nodes

Key Results

  • Strong performance across 8 benchmark datasets
  • Outperforms persistent homology baselines in speed and accuracy
  • Enables 2D visualization of graph datasets
  • Scalable to large datasets such as OGBG-MOLHIV
